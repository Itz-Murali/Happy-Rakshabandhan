The Festival

About Raksha Bandhan

Raksha Bandhan, also known as Rakhi, is a sacred Hindu festival celebrating the timeless bond between brothers and sisters. On this day, sisters tie a colorful rakhi — a thread of protection — around their brothers' wrists, praying for their well-being, while brothers vow to protect and cherish their sisters through every season of life.

The Sacred Rituals

The Thread

The rakhi is tied with love — a symbol of protection, prayer and an unspoken vow.

The Aarti

A glowing diya circles the wrist, blessing the bond with light and good fortune.

The Sweets

Mouths are sweetened with mithai, sealing the moment with joy and warmth.

The Gift

Brothers offer a token of love — a promise to always stand by their sister's side.

Some bonds are not tied by blood, but by moments, care, and quiet understanding — and this thread will keep us close, always.
